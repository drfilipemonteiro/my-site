About Me

Hello! I am a researcher at Observatório Nacional, Brazil, acting mainly in the following topics: physical and dynamical properties of small Solar System bodies; photometry and polarimetry of small bodies; origin and evolution of near-Earth asteroids; physical characterization and formation of binary asteroids; and asteroid mineralogy and its correspondence with meteorites.

I am part of the Grupo de Ciências Planetárias and the IMPACTON project team, performing observations at the Observatório Astronômico do Sertão de Itaparica (OASI). Previously, I obtained a degree in Physics from the Universidade Federal Rural de Pernambuco (UFRPE) and a master's and doctorate in Astronomy from the Observatório Nacional (ON).

I was a postdoctoral fellow with the Programa de Capacitação Institucional (PCI/ON) and a FAPERJ Nota 10 postdoctoral fellow at ON. In 2024, I worked as a postdoctoral researcher at the Laboratório Nacional de Astrofísica (LNA/MCTI), contributing to the development team of the SPARC4 instrument pipeline. In 2025, I was a postdoctoral researcher at the Universidade Estadual Paulista (UNESP).

In 2023, I was honored by the International Astronomical Union (IAU) with the naming of asteroid (34586) Filipemonteiro. I am a member of the Sociedade Astronômica Brasileira (SAB) and the International Astronomical Union (IAU).

Asteroid Filipemonteiro (2000 SK352)

Overview

  • Official designation: 34586 Filipemonteiro (2000 SK352)
  • Category: Main-belt asteroid
  • Discovered on September 30, 2000, by the LONEOS program at Anderson Mesa
  • Estimated diameter: ~5.5 km
  • Absolute magnitude: 13.6

No Close Approaches

  • Minimum Earth distance: 1.85 AU
  • Not considered a Near-Earth Object
  • Risk classification: Not potentially hazardous

Accessibility and Exploration

  • Not listed as a viable target for human missions (NHATS)
  • Similar objects: 35 Leukothea, 61 Danae, 117 Lomia

Orbital Elements

  • Semi-major axis: 2.99 AU
  • Perihelion: 2.86 AU
  • Aphelion: 3.13 AU
  • Eccentricity: 0.0445
  • Orbital inclination: 11.53°
  • Orbital period: 5.17 years (1,890 days)

Physical Characteristics

  • Likely composition: rocky (typical main-belt asteroid)
  • Diameter: ~5.5 km
  • Comparison: similar in size to San Francisco Bay

Derived Characteristics

  • First recorded observation: March 24, 1998
  • Last recorded observation: January 24, 2023
  • Total observations: 1,684 (Minor Planet Center)
